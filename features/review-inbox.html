The private feedback flow

Some customers just need to be heard — preferably not in public. Our feedback flow gives unhappy customers a private way to tell you what went wrong before they head to Google. You get the message directly, fix it with a call or a redo, and most of the time the public one-star never happens.

It's not about hiding from criticism — negative reviews that do get posted still get answered, promptly and professionally. It's about giving problems a chance to be solved first.

Owners ask us

Which review sites do you cover?

Sixty-seven and counting — Google, Facebook, Yelp, TripAdvisor, plus the booking apps and directories specific to each industry, from Booksy and Fresha to Zocdoc, RepairPal, Thumbtack, and OpenTable. See the full list. If a site matters to your business and it’s not on it, we’ll request it.

What about Yelp?

We monitor Yelp closely and reply to every Yelp review — but we don't send customers there to leave reviews, because Yelp's own guidelines discourage businesses from soliciting them. Your asks focus on Google and your industry's sites, where requested reviews are welcome, and Yelp grows organically.

Do I have to check the inbox?

No — that's the point. We check it. You get alerts for the things worth knowing about (a negative review, a private feedback message) and a monthly report for everything else. If you like logging in and reading everything, you're welcome to — but you never have to.
