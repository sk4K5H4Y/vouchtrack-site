The new "page one" is three sentences long

On Google, being result #6 still gets you seen. When someone asks an AI assistant, there is no #6 — the answer names a couple of businesses and moves on. What decides who gets named? Largely the same things that win local search: a strong rating, a steady flow of recent reviews, reviews that mention what you're great at, and consistent business information across the web.

That's not luck. It's a profile you can build deliberately — which is exactly what VouchTrack does all day.

What we actually do

  • Test what ChatGPT, Gemini & other assistants say when asked for businesses like yours in your area
  • Track it monthly — are you named? Who is instead?
  • Strengthen the signals AI relies on: review volume, recency, rating, and what your reviews actually say

An honest note

Nobody can guarantee an AI assistant will recommend your business — anyone promising that is selling something. What we can do is measure where you stand, show you the trend, and relentlessly improve the inputs AI systems are known to draw on. The businesses with the strongest review profiles keep showing up in the answers. That's the game, and it's winnable.

Owners ask us

Which AI assistants do you track?

The major ones people actually use to find local businesses — ChatGPT and Gemini today, expanding as new assistants earn real usage. The signals that win one tend to win the others.
