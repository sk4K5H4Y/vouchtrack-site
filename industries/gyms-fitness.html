After milestone moments — first month completed, a class streak, a PR — members get a text in your gym’s name: “One month strong! If you’re loving it here, mind sharing it on Google?” One tap, review done.

How it works in a gym or studio

Milestones trigger asks

New-member onboarding, streaks, and wins you define automatically trigger review requests by text or email — asked when pride is at its peak, never at random.

Reviews roll in

Members who love their gym recruit for it — reviews are just recruiting in writing. Asking consistently converts locker-room loyalty into public proof.

January fills up

When resolution season hits, your rating, review volume, and “beginners welcome” stories make you the gym people commit to — and the profile keeps working all year.

Gym owners ask us

When’s the right moment to ask a member?

After wins, not visits — the end of a first month, a class-streak milestone, a personal record. You define the moments; the system asks automatically and never nags: members are asked occasionally and only until they’ve reviewed.

What about angry cancellation reviews?

Cancellation friction is the classic gym review problem. The private feedback flow catches frustrated members before they go public, and the cancellations that do review get calm, graceful replies — which prospective members read as “this place will treat me fairly too.”

Do requests come from my gym’s name?

Yes — every text and email is in your gym’s name and voice, coach energy included if that’s your brand. Members never see VouchTrack anywhere.
