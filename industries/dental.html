Built with patient privacy in mind

Healthcare is different, and we treat it that way. Review requests are worded carefully and generically — no treatment details, no health information, ever. Responses to reviews never confirm or discuss anyone's status as a patient or their care, in line with HIPAA obligations.

  • Post-visit review requests by text or email — carefully worded, privacy-safe
  • Every review answered promptly with compliant, warm, professional responses
  • Private feedback channel so concerns reach your front desk before they reach Google
  • Your best reviews displayed on your practice website for anxious searchers to find

The compounding effect for practices

A practice seeing 300 patients a month that captures reviews from even 10% adds 30 reviews monthly — 360 a year. Within two years, you're not just the highest-rated practice in your area. You're the obviously highest-rated one.

And because dental relationships last decades, every new patient a strong review profile brings in is worth thousands in lifetime value.

Practice managers ask us

Is automated review requesting HIPAA-safe?

Review requests contain no health information — they're a simple, generic thank-you with a review link, which is standard practice across healthcare. Review responses are written to never confirm patient status or discuss care. We'll walk your compliance officer through the full flow on the demo call if you'd like.

Our front desk is already stretched thin.

That's the point — nothing here adds to their plate. Requests send automatically after appointments, responses are drafted for one-click approval, and your monthly report arrives by email. Zero new workload.

What about the one bad review that's haunting us?

Two answers. If it violates Google's policies, we flag it for removal. If it's real, the strategy is volume: a steady stream of fresh 5-star reviews pushes old negatives down and drowns them out. Most practices see that one bad review lose all visibility within 60–90 days.
