PT clinic owners ask us

Is this compatible with HIPAA?

Asking for reviews is fine — patients decide what they share. The risk sits in the replies, and ours are HIPAA-aware by default: no confirming someone was a patient, no conditions, no treatment details, and a human approves every reply before it posts.

When is the right time to ask a PT patient?

Not after visit one — after wins. We time requests to milestone sessions and discharge, when progress is concrete and gratitude is at its peak. You define the trigger points during setup; the system handles the rest forever.

Do physician referrals make reviews unnecessary?

The opposite: referred patients still look you up, and a thin profile sends them to the hospital-owned clinic with the familiar name. Reviews are how an independent practice converts the referrals it already earns.
