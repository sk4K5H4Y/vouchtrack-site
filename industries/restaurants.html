Great food deserves a great rating

Restaurants get reviewed whether they like it or not — the question is whether your happiest guests are part of the conversation. Most aren't: they enjoy the meal, tell their friends, and never think to post. Meanwhile the one table with a long wait writes three paragraphs. VouchTrack fixes the imbalance.

  • QR codes on receipts, table tents, and takeout bags — a review is one scan away
  • Follow-up requests to online-order and reservation customers by text or email
  • Every review answered — praise thanked, complaints handled gracefully in public
  • Review monitoring across Google, Yelp, Facebook, and food platforms in one inbox

The volume advantage

Restaurants have something no other business has: hundreds of happy customers every single week. Even a small capture rate makes you the fastest-growing review profile in your neighborhood.

A restaurant serving 800 covers a week that converts just 3% adds 100+ reviews a month. That pace buries old complaints, lifts your rating, and keeps you at the top of "restaurants near me" — where full tables come from.

Restaurant owners ask us

We don't collect customer phone numbers at the table.

You don't need to. For dine-in, the QR code on the check does the work. For online orders, reservations, and loyalty signups — where you do have contact info — automatic text and email requests kick in. The two together cover nearly every guest.

Yelp is brutal to restaurants. Can you help there too?

Yes — all your Yelp reviews flow into the same inbox and every one gets a response. One note: Yelp's rules don't allow directly asking customers for Yelp reviews, so our requests point to Google (where the rules allow it and where most diners search anyway) while we monitor and respond everywhere.

A competitor is clearly posting fake reviews about us.

Reviews that violate Google's policies — fake accounts, competitors, people who never dined with you — can be flagged for removal, and we manage that process for you, including the follow-up when Google is slow.
