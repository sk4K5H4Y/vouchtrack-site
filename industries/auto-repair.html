Independent shops beat the chains on honesty, skill, and price. But the chains have hundreds of reviews and you have a few dozen — so the driver with a check-engine light calls them first. That's not a fairness problem you can argue with. It's a numbers problem you can fix.

Why this matters more in auto repair

Repair customers don't come back weekly like a salon client — you might see them twice a year. That means every single visit has to count as a review opportunity, because there aren't many of them.

A shop serving 150 cars a month that captures reviews from even 15% of them adds 20+ reviews monthly. Within a year, you're the most-reviewed shop in your zip code — and the first call for every new driver in town.

Shop owners ask us

My counter guys won't remember to ask for reviews.

They don't have to. VouchTrack sends the request automatically after each ticket closes — no one on your team has to remember anything. The QR code on the counter is there as a bonus for customers paying in person.

I got a nasty review from someone we never even serviced. Can you help?

Yes. Reviews that violate Google's policies (wrong business, fake, spam) can be flagged for removal, and we handle that process. For unfair-but-real reviews, a calm, factual response wins — future customers judge you by how you reply, not by the complaint.

Do texts work for my customers? A lot of them are older.

Texts have the highest response rate across every age group — but we send by email too, and the counter QR code covers everyone else. We tune the mix to your customer base during setup.
