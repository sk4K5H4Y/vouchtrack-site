Chiropractors ask us

Is asking patients for reviews okay under HIPAA?

Asking is fine — patients choose freely what to share about themselves. The compliance risk lives in the replies, and ours are HIPAA-aware by default: they never confirm someone was a patient, never mention conditions or treatment, and a human approves every one before it posts.

My patients come on referral. Do reviews still matter?

Referred patients still Google you before booking — a referral gets you considered, and your profile gets you chosen. Strong reviews also turn one referral into several, because the friend who was told about you shows the profile to their spouse.

What about patients who quit their care plan?

Our private feedback flow gives frustrated patients a direct way to tell you first, so a scheduling or billing gripe becomes a phone call instead of a public two-star. Public negatives that do appear get calm, professional replies that reassure everyone else reading.
