Good to know

Is asking customers for reviews legal at all?

Yes — asking for honest reviews is allowed and encouraged. The trouble starts with how some businesses ask: paying for reviews, filtering who gets asked based on how happy they seem, requesting a specific star rating, or posting reviews themselves. That's what this checkup screens for.

What is review gating?

Review gating is pre-screening customers — usually with a "how was your experience?" survey — and only showing the Google review link to the happy ones. Google's review policies prohibit it. A private feedback path for unhappy customers is fine, as long as everyone can still reach the public review page.

What happens if Google catches a violation?

Consequences range from the offending reviews being removed, to review functionality being restricted, to the business profile being suspended — and rebuilding from a suspension is far harder than building right the first time. FTC rule violations can additionally carry civil penalties.
